Maybe the world isn't so bad afterall

I already believe that the whole world does not suck. I think we all live here for a reason. It would suck if we were brought into this world only to see it decay. There are some hefty reasons for why I want to talk about this. One is that it seems that news outlets (not that I watch the news, but rather what I hear about it in class and from peers) and social media tend to exagerate and polarize its viewers. I already heard this was a problem, but I didn't know the extend of it until today. When I am on instagram, if I am scrolling, I typically spend more time looking at positive things that are happening throughout the world, such as today, I read that a certain location has found the antidote for depression. Another thing I read today is that Trump recently signed papers that aim to lower animal suffering and abuse. This made me wonder. After all, I have heard so much bad things about him. Were they all wrong? I then did some calculations, because I only just put two and two together. Although I got straight A's through most of my high school career, taking multiple AP social studies classes, I somehow managed to entirely disregard the fact that America is still a baby. No, I'm not saying that the citizens are young; in fact, I know a lot of old people. What I am saying is that America only became America in 1776. To me, this sounds like a long long long long looooooooong time ago, but I decided to do some calculations, and as it turns out, 2026 - 1776 = 250. America is only 250 years old! For probably the first third, America was too busy doubling and tripling its size, fighting other nations, and creating social hierarchies. It faced racism and sexism nonstop. Eventually, in the way later half, president LBJ passed the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and the Voting Rights Act of 1965. Those aforementioned issues were so apparent that even the 36th president had to deal with it. But hey! It gets better. Upon further calculations, I see that this was all only 61 years ago. The Emancipation Proclomation, which "freed" the slaves, was signed 163 years ago. This means that change was happening, but only at an extraordinarily slow pace. Now, things are happening faster (I think). We created better health cares, better educations, better lifestyles for ordinary schmoes like us. We also created abortions and legalized it too, in 1973. This was 53 years ago. Wow! How fast! Unfortunately, there is a massive debate about keeping/losing the right to an abortion. I thought this meant that we were on a bad path, and maybe we are, but in the grand scheme of things, I think our path as a whole is a net positive, logistic growth. Even if we do lose the right of abortions or contraceptives, I'm sure we'll get it back. In the end, I am sure we will still move in the right direction. Call me nieve, but I think it will get better. June 20, 2026. As always, -ai.
